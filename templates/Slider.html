Aghori Babas of Ujjain

Aghori Babas are ascetic Shaiva sadhus known for their extreme and unconventional spiritual practices. Ujjain, a holy city in Madhya Pradesh, is one of the significant places where Aghori traditions have flourished.

Spiritual Practices

Aghoris in Ujjain are known for their intense meditation practices, often performed in cremation grounds. They believe in transcending the duality of life and death, seeking to attain spiritual enlightenment through unconventional means.

Mahakala Temple

The Mahakala Temple in Ujjain is a significant site for Aghori Babas. This ancient temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva, is one of the 12 Jyotirlingas and holds great importance in Aghori traditions and practices.

Rituals and Customs

Aghori Babas in Ujjain perform various unique rituals, including the use of human ash, meditation with corpses, and consuming intoxicants. These practices are aimed at breaking societal taboos and achieving a state of non-duality.

Modern Perceptions

While often misunderstood, Aghori Babas of Ujjain continue to fascinate both devotees and researchers. Their extreme practices challenge conventional notions of spirituality, offering a unique perspective on life, death, and the pursuit of enlightenment.
